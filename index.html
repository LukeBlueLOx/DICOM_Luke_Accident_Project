EN: Ladies and Gentlemen - On May 1 2023, just before 8 am GMT+2 CEST, I had a rather serious bicycle accident. Leaving section 46 of the "FENDER" route - the "GIANTS" Bicycle Range on a forest, mountain asphalt road: when approaching the bend before leaving the "straight line", I lowered my head only for a moment to check the position of the derailleurs if they were in the highest gear. Coming out of the bend, my reaction was therefore minimally delayed to take even earlier braking before the closed barrier. My surprise was huge at the sight of this obstacle on the road, it was already fractions of a second and all I had to do was try to brake, inevitably approaching the barrier. I estimate that I managed to reduce the speed by half - unfortunately the braking was so strong just before the barrier that in the last phase I was heading towards it with quite high speed on the front wheel - which unfortunately ended with my left cheek hitting the barrier with a force. I suffered quite serious facial injuries - but it seems that the worst is behind me: check-ups, sutures removal, rest and full recovery. I will share the descriptions of the injuries later in my presentation. Below is a map showing an illustrative proposal to place just before this unlucky bend - two folded plastic warning signs about a closed barrier - in a glaring orange color: these signs could be put up by the services responsible for closing this barrier on both sides of the road - for the duration of its closing . I don't blame anyone here - I went a bit by heart, because I was used to: mostly open road in this place. As I guessed after the accident itself, the closed barrier was probably related to the greater influx of tourists on May 1 to limit car traffic in the mountains, which I also mentioned to all the people helping me. It is worth taking the initiative to place inexpensive fold-out plastic warning signs there for the time the barrier is closed - because it can really help in the future to avoid someone's death, an even more serious accident than mine, injury, pain, losses and costs.
